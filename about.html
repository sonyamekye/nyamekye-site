About

A technologist's discipline. A writer's patience.

Two habits run through everything Stephen builds: verify before you trust, and say only what needs saying.

Stephen Okyere Nyamekye is a cybersecurity specialist working in information security management, based in Ghana. He holds a bachelor's degree in Information Technology and is currently pursuing a master's degree in Cybersecurity — a combination that keeps him equally comfortable in a threat model and a classroom.

Alongside his security work, Stephen is a freelance writer and ghostwriter, an educator, and an entrepreneur. His projects tend to share a structure: research something thoroughly, organize it so someone else can actually use it, then publish it — whether that's a cybersecurity awareness series, a full academic curriculum, or a book.

His most extensive current project is a full Grade 4 Science resource suite for Ghanaian schools — a scheme of work, a 35-chapter teacher manual, companion worksheets, and a student textbook, aligned to the GES academic calendar and a Cambridge Primary Science hybrid syllabus, written chapter by chapter with Ghanaian context woven throughout.

Stephen's Ghanaian heritage — including Akan traditions and Ashanti cultural systems — regularly informs his creative and educational work, giving his writing and teaching materials a grounding that's specific rather than generic.

He is the published author of I Build in Silence Whiles They Thought I Was Losing, available on Amazon KDP, and continues to develop supporting material around it.
