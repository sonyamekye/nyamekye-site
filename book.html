I Build in Silence Whiles They Thought I Was Losing

There's a particular kind of progress that doesn't announce itself — the version of building that happens while everyone else is only watching for a visible result. This book is about that stretch: the work done quietly, on a timeline nobody else can see, by someone who kept going without needing an audience to confirm it was working.

Drawing on personal experience and observation, Stephen writes for anyone in a season that looks like standing still from the outside but is, underneath, under construction.
